# Read the Signal: 45-minute Session Plan

## Duration

45 minutes.

## Audience

Teachers, science communicators, workshop facilitators, student groups, and developer reviewers.

## Prerequisites

Browser-only participants need a browser.
Local technical participants need Git, Node/Corepack, Python, and the OpenPlazma repository.

## Materials

- Public demo: `https://mishima-computing.github.io/openplazma/`
- Participant handout.
- Facilitator guide.
- Safety and scope notes.
- Optional local technical appendix.

## Learning Goals

- Identify a visible Signal change.
- Separate observation from hypothesis.
- Understand that the task uses `STATIC_FIXTURE` data only.
- Understand that a Run is a recorded local workflow.
- Understand that Observatory and Compare inspect local records.
- Understand read-only decision support: evidence, assumptions, limitations, and comparison without equipment control.

## Session Timeline

0-5 min: Mission Briefing and safety/scope.

- State the Workshop Boundary.
- Explain that this is a read-only evidence Mission using `STATIC_FIXTURE` data.

5-10 min: Enter the Lab.

- Open the public demo.
- Confirm the Lab and Signal are visible.

10-18 min: Read the Signal.

- Ask participants to describe visible changes.
- Keep observation separate from explanation.

18-25 min: Write observation and hypothesis.

- Use the participant handout.
- Keep the Logbook reflective, not evaluative.

25-32 min: Local guided workflow demo or facilitator demo.

- Optional technical path.
- Show how the guided StudyFlow creates local Runs.

32-38 min: Observatory and Compare demo.

- Show local Run inspection.
- Show a two-Run Compare page.

38-45 min: Debrief and feedback.

- Ask what participants observed.
- Ask what Observatory and Compare helped them notice.
- Collect feedback without requesting private personal data.

## Facilitator Notes

- Use Mission language to keep the session approachable.
- Keep boundary language visible and concise.
- Let participants say what they see before discussing hypotheses.
- Treat the local technical path as optional.

## Optional Extensions

- Repeat the Mission locally and compare two Runs.
- Ask participants to choose one Metric and explain why it helped.
- Ask reviewers to note confusing terms for documentation follow-up.

## What Not To Do

- Do not add grading or scoring.
- Do not use correctness rubrics.
- Do not ask participants to operate hardware.
- Do not describe public data ingestion as available.
- Do not imply command/control, hazardous procedure generation, or standalone safety-critical design authority.
